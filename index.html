Kathmandu - The Capital of Nepal

Kathmandu is the capital and largest city of Nepal, rich in history, culture, and heritage. Dating back over 2,000 years, it was a key center of trade and religion in the ancient Kathmandu Valley. The city is famous for its medieval temples, palaces, and courtyards, influenced by Hindu and Buddhist traditions.

Historical Highlights

  • The Malla Dynasty (12th–18th century) shaped much of the city's architecture
  • In 1768, King Prithvi Narayan Shah unified Nepal, making Kathmandu the capital
  • Preserved heritage despite modern urbanization and the 2015 earthquake

Tourist Attractions

  • Durbar Square – UNESCO World Heritage site with palaces and temples
  • Swayambhunath – Sacred Buddhist stupa with panoramic views
  • Pashupatinath Temple – Revered Hindu temple on Bagmati River
  • Boudhanath Stupa – One of the world's largest stupas
  • Thamel – Vibrant tourist area with shops and nightlife

Kathmandu offers a unique blend of history, spirituality, and adventure, making it a must-visit destination in South Asia.
